## **INTRODUCTION** 

## **Project Context** 

Across the world, religious communities continue to organize large-scale faithbased programs that require strong coordination, timely announcements, and effective resource management. Major religious observances such as Ramadan, Easter, and Diwali involve a wide range of activities including prayer schedules, charity distributions, gatherings, and community-based events (Mohammed et al., 2023). These activities often require coordination among religious leaders, volunteers, donors, and local organizations. As communities grow larger, organizing these programs becomes more complex, especially when multiple events occur simultaneously in different locations. 

Globally, many communities have shifted toward the use of web-based event management systems to reduce manual workload and improve planning efficiency. Digital systems allow organizations to schedule events, send real-time announcements, track attendance, and monitor donation distributions (Sukmandhani et al., 2022). Studies show that web-based platforms enhance engagement and coordination by providing a centralized space for communication and documentation. Garcia and Santos (2021) emphasized that interactive web-based systems improve stakeholder participation by allowing organized planning and efficient service delivery. This global trend highlights the increasing importance of digital solutions in managing community programs (Kukartsev et al., 2023). 

In the Philippines, the growing adoption of digital platforms has become evident across government agencies, institutions, and community organizations. The Department of Trade and Industry (DTI, 2022) emphasized that digital transformation is increasingly being adopted to streamline organizational processes, improve service delivery, and reduce inefficiencies caused by manual operations. As more Filipino communities become connected through internet access and mobile technology, digital systems have become a practical solution for coordination and public service management (Torres & Bautista, 2024). 

In Muslim communities across Mindanao and other parts of the Philippines, Ramadan remains one of the most important religious seasons. It involves daily fasting, prayer, charity activities, and community gatherings. According to the National

Despite this growth, many communities still rely on informal and manual approaches in managing Ramadan programs, resulting in operational challenges and limited documentation (Aldrin et al., 2024). 

In General Santos City, the Muslim community actively observes Ramadan through various barangay and mosque-level programs such as Iftar gatherings, Taraweeh prayer schedules, charity distributions, religious announcements, and coordination with local government units. These challenges affect the efficiency and transparency of program implementation, especially when multiple events occur within the same time period. 

The lack of a centralized platform limits accessibility for elderly participants and residents in remote areas who may not consistently receive online announcements or informal updates. The absence of an organized system also makes it difficult for barangay officials and community leaders to monitor event status, confirm attendance, and produce accurate reports for evaluation and planning (Kumar & Priya, 2025). These local challenges emphasize the need for a centralized web-based event management system that can improve scheduling, communication, monitoring, and documentation of Ramadanrelated programs in General Santos City.

## **Purpose and Description** 

The purpose of the proposed system is to provide a centralized and structured digital platform that improves the management, coordination, and documentation of Ramadan-related community activities. The system is designed to enhance collaboration among LGU/Barangay Admins, Imams/Mosque Administrators, Community Leaders, and Community Viewers by integrating scheduling, notification, monitoring, and reporting functionalities into a single web-based application. It aims to reduce reliance on manual processes, improve information accuracy, and streamline communication among stakeholders. 

The system enables authorized users to create and manage event schedules, including assigned venues, organizers, and time details, with a calendar view for better visualization. It includes an announcement and notification module for posting updates and sending alerts to stakeholders. Community leaders and Imams can input prayer schedules, mosque events, and charitable activities for centralized documentation. 

Additionally, the system provides activity monitoring and attendance tracking features to record participation and track event status. A resource and donation management module supports the recording of donations and distributions, ensuring transparency. The system also generates printable reports, including event schedules, attendance summaries, and donation records. 

Overall, the system enhances service delivery by improving organization, communication, and transparency. By utilizing web-based technologies accessible through internet-connected devices, the system promotes accessibility and efficiency, leading to improved coordination and community engagement during the Ramadan season.
