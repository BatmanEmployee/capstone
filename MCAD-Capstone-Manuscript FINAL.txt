INTRODUCTION

Project Context

      The Muslim community in General Santos City, like many across the Philippines, observes Ramadan as the most sacred season of the year. It is marked by fasting, prayer, charity, and community gatherings. Ramadan involves a wide range of organized programs at the barangay and mosque levels, including Iftar events, Taraweeh prayer schedules, charitable distributions, community announcements, and coordination with local government officials. According to the National Commission on Muslim Filipinos (NCMF, 2022), Muslim communities in Mindanao continue to grow in number and organizational complexity, reinforcing the need for structured coordination mechanisms during major Islamic observances.
      Despite the increasing number of Ramadan-related activities, many barangays and Muslim community organizations in General Santos City still rely on manual and informal processes to manage these programs. Common tools include paper-based scheduling, social media postings, messaging applications, and verbal announcements. While these methods provide basic coordination, they often result in schedule conflicts, delayed announcements, incomplete monitoring, and difficulties in tracking donations and resource distributions. The absence of a centralized platform limits the efficient management of time-sensitive programs involving multiple stakeholders such as barangay officials, Imams, community leaders, and residents.
      Globally, the adoption of web-based systems for community and event management has been shown to reduce manual workload and improve coordination. Garcia and Santos (2021) stated that interactive web-based systems enhance stakeholder engagement by enabling active participation in planning and service delivery. In the Philippine context, the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI, 2022) highlighted the increasing adoption of digital platforms among government and community organizations to streamline operations.
      Findings from the data gathering further confirm that current practices rely on messaging applications, paper-based invitations, and social media postings for scheduling and announcements. Attendance is recorded manually, while donation and distribution records are also managed through manual or informal methods. These practices lead to challenges such as difficulty in confirming participant attendance, inefficiencies in coordination, and limited accessibility for elderly participants and those in remote areas. These findings emphasize the need for a centralized web-based system to improve organization, communication, and documentation of Ramadan community programs.
      
      
      

Purpose and Description

       The purpose of the proposed system is to provide a centralized and structured digital platform that improves the management, coordination, and documentation of Ramadan-related community activities. The system is designed to enhance collaboration among LGU/Barangay Admins, Imams/Mosque Administrators, Community Leaders, and Community Viewers by integrating scheduling, notification, monitoring, and reporting functionalities into a single web-based application. It aims to reduce reliance on manual processes, improve information accuracy, and streamline communication among stakeholders.
      The system enables authorized users to create and manage event schedules, including assigned venues, organizers, and time details, with a calendar view for better visualization. It includes an announcement and notification module for posting updates and sending alerts to stakeholders. Community leaders and Imams can input prayer schedules, mosque events, and charitable activities for centralized documentation.
      Additionally, the system provides activity monitoring and attendance tracking features to record participation and track event status. A resource and donation management module supports the recording of donations and distributions, ensuring transparency. The system also generates printable reports, including event schedules, attendance summaries, and donation records.
      Overall, the system enhances service delivery by improving organization, communication, and transparency. By utilizing web-based technologies accessible through internet-connected devices, the system promotes accessibility and efficiency, leading to improved coordination and community engagement during the Ramadan season.
      



Objectives

General Objective

      To design and develop Web-Based Event Management System with Community Forum For City Mayor's Office Muslim Concerns and Affairs Division., a web-based scheduling, notification, and activity monitoring system that will improve the coordination, communication, and documentation of Ramadan season programs and community services in General Santos City.

Specific Objectives


1. Develop a web-based event management system with role-based access for LGU/Barangay Admin, Imam/Mosque Administrator, Community Leader, and Community Viewer, ensuring secure and centralized management of Ramadan programs.
2. Design and implement an integrated scheduling, announcement, and notification module that supports efficient event planning, real-time information dissemination, and coordinated communication among stakeholders.
3. Develop an activity monitoring, attendance tracking, and resource management module that records event participation, tracks event status, and documents donations and distributions for transparent program management.
4. Generate automated, printable, and exportable reports, including event schedules, attendance summaries, and donation records, to support evaluation and decision-making by barangay officials.



Scope and Limitations

The scope of the project covers the development of Web-Based Event Management System with Community Forum For City Mayor's Office Muslim Concerns and Affairs Division. The system is designed to improve program scheduling, announcement dissemination, activity monitoring, and donation tracking for Ramadan-related community programs. The System aims to enhance coordination, communication, and transparency among its four primary users: LGU/Barangay Admin, Imam/Mosque Administrator, Community Leader, and Community Viewer. By integrating these modules into a single centralized platform, the system minimizes reliance on manual and informal tools, improves data organization, and ensures a more efficient workflow for Ramadan program management.

The following were the scopes of the project:
1. User Management Module
	The system supports four user roles with role-based access control:
• LGU/Barangay Admin – manages the entire system, approves accounts, and generates reports.
• Imam/Mosque Administrator – inputs prayer schedules and mosque-related events.
• Community Leader – submits and monitors community Ramadan activities.
• Community Viewer – views published announcements and schedules.

2. Ramadan Program and Activity Scheduling Module
• Create, edit, and delete Ramadan events such as Iftar programs, Taraweeh schedules, and charity drives.
• Assign event details including title, date and time, venue, organizer, and description.
• Calendar view and list view for event schedules.
• Event status tracking categorized as upcoming, ongoing, or completed.
3. Announcement and Notification Module
• Post official announcements categorized as prayer schedules, event reminders, charity drive updates, or community advisories.
• System notification alerts for registered users.
• Optional email notification feature for stakeholder dissemination.
4. Activity Monitoring and Attendance Tracking Module
• Attendance list input per event.
• Event monitoring dashboard showing event status and participation statistics.
• Generate simple event monitoring reports.
5. Resource and Donation Management Module
• Record donations received including donor name (optional), donation type (cash, food, or supplies), amount or quantity, and date received.
• Record distribution logs including beneficiary, items distributed, and date.
• Donation and distribution summary dashboard.
6. Report Generation Module
• Generate and export printable reports including Ramadan event schedule reports, attendance summaries, and donation and distribution records.
• Export options via PDF or browser print function.
7. Dashboard and Analytics
• Summary dashboard showing total number of events, upcoming events, attendance totals, and donation totals.

The limitations of the study are as follows:

1. System Access Limitation: Web-Based Event Management System with Community Forum for City Mayor's Office Muslim Concerns and Affairs Division. is a web-based application and requires a stable internet connection and a compatible web browser. Offline functionality is not supported.
2. Mobile Application Limitation: The system is web-only and mobile-responsive but does not include a dedicated Android or iOS mobile application.
3. Notification Limitation: Real-time SMS gateway integration is not included unless a free API is available during development. Notifications are limited to in-system alerts and optional email notifications.
4. Attendance Tracking Limitation: Biometric attendance tracking and QR code scanning are not included in the base scope and are considered optional features subject to time availability.
5. Islamic Calendar Limitation: Automated Islamic calendar computation is not included. All event dates and schedules are entered manually by the authorized administrator.
6. Payment Processing Limitation: The system does not support online payment processing for donations. All donation records are manually entered by authorized users.
7. Data Accuracy Limitation: The accuracy of event schedules, attendance records, and donation logs is dependent on correct and timely input from authorized users. Incorrect data entry may affect the reliability of generated reports.

METHODOLOGY


Figure 1: Agile-Kanban Methodology Mode

       The methodology used in developing the proposed system focuses on creating a user-centered, efficient, and scalable web application. The system is designed to improve scheduling, announcement dissemination, activity monitoring, and donation tracking for Ramadan community programs in General Santos City.
      
      To achieve this, the researchers adopted the Agile Kanban methodology, which supports continuous development, flexibility, and adaptation to changing user requirements. This approach allows the team to develop the system in manageable tasks while continuously testing and improving each feature. Key components such as event scheduling, notification management, attendance tracking, and donation recording were developed iteratively to ensure functionality, usability, and reliability.
      
      The development process began with a planning phase, where functional and non-functional requirements were identified through interviews and consultations with barangay staff, Imams, and community leaders. Functional requirements included event management, announcement posting, attendance tracking, donation recording, and report generation, while non-functional requirements focused on usability, performance, and security.
      A Kanban board was used to manage tasks, categorized into “To Do,” “In Progress,” and “Done,” allowing the team to monitor progress and address issues efficiently. Continuous testing and user feedback were incorporated throughout development to ensure that the system meets user needs and operates effectively.

      Agile Kanban was chosen for this project because it provides a visual, flexible, and efficient framework for managing software development. The system was developed in stages across the four main user modules — Barangay Admin, Imam, Community Leader, and Community Viewer — while continuously integrating feedback into the workflow. The use of a Kanban board enabled the team to track tasks, monitor progress, and manage priorities effectively, ensuring that development stayed aligned with project objectives and timelines. Through this approach, the system remained iterative and adaptable, allowing continuous improvement of features such as event scheduling, notification delivery, activity monitoring, and report generation.
      The development process began with a comprehensive planning phase, where the researchers identified and analyzed both functional and non-functional requirements of the system through interviews and consultations with barangay staff, Imams, and community leaders. Functional requirements included event creation and management, announcement posting, attendance recording, donation logging, and report generation, while non-functional requirements focused on usability, system performance, and data security. These requirements were translated into detailed user stories that reflected the needs of all user roles. Each user story was then divided into smaller, actionable tasks to ensure clarity and efficient implementation.
      Following the planning stage, a Kanban board was established as the main tool for visualizing the development workflow. The board was organized into columns such as To Do, In Progress, and Done, with each task represented by a card that moved across these stages as work progressed. This setup allowed the team to clearly monitor task status, track overall development progress, and quickly identify delays or bottlenecks. It also improved transparency and accountability, as all team members had real-time visibility of the project workflow.
      During the execution phase, the team adopted a pull-based approach by selecting tasks from the To Do column based on priority and team capacity. Developers worked collaboratively to implement system features, including the Admin module for system and user management, the scheduling and notification modules for event coordination, and the monitoring and donation modules for activity tracking and resource documentation. Continuous integration and testing were conducted to ensure that each feature functioned correctly and met the defined acceptance criteria.
      After completing development tasks, the system underwent testing and evaluation to validate its functionality, performance, and usability. The researchers simulated real-world Ramadan program management scenarios by acting as Barangay Admins, Imams, and Community Leaders to test the system's full workflow. Admins tested user management, event scheduling, announcement posting, and report generation. Community leaders tested attendance recording and donation entry, while Imams tested prayer schedule input and event monitoring. Tasks that passed testing were marked as completed and moved to the Done column.
      User feedback was gathered through system demonstrations and evaluations, which played a crucial role in refining the platform. Barangay staff found the centralized scheduling and notification features helpful in improving coordination. Community leaders appreciated the donation tracking and report generation capabilities, while Imams valued the organized schedule management for prayer times and mosque events. Suggestions for improvement were incorporated into subsequent development cycles through the updated product backlog.
      Finally, the team engaged in continuous improvement through regular evaluation and retrospective activities to identify strengths, weaknesses, and possible enhancements. The product backlog was continuously updated to incorporate new features, improvements, and user feedback, ensuring that the system remained relevant, efficient, and user-friendly throughout the development process.


Technical Background


Technologies to be Used

      Web-Based Event Management System with Community Forum For City Mayor's Office Muslim Concerns and Affairs Division was developed using standard and widely adopted web technologies to ensure accessibility, reliability, and ease of deployment. The frontend of the system was created using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, which are essential technologies for building interactive and responsive web applications. HTML was used to structure the web pages, CSS was applied to design and enhance the visual presentation, and JavaScript was utilized to implement dynamic features and improve user interaction. The development environment used was Visual Studio Code (VS Code), which provided a flexible and efficient platform for coding, debugging, and managing project files.

      On the back-end, the system was developed using PHP, a server-side scripting language that enables the processing of user requests, handling of business logic, and communication with the database. PHP was used to manage core system functionalities such as user authentication, event scheduling logic, announcement posting, attendance recording, donation entry management, and report generation. This allowed the system to dynamically generate content and respond to user actions in real time.
      For data storage and management, the system utilized MySQL, a relational database management system known for its reliability and efficiency. MySQL was used to store and organize important system data including user accounts, event schedules, attendance records, donation logs, distribution records, and announcement content. The integration of PHP and MySQL enabled efficient data retrieval, updating, and management, ensuring data consistency and system reliability throughout the Ramadan program period.
      The system also incorporates responsive web design principles to ensure accessibility across desktop and mobile devices, which is particularly important for community users who primarily access digital platforms through smartphones. Each user role was provided with a role-based dashboard designed to support their specific tasks, such as system management for the Barangay Admin, event input for the Imam, activity coordination for the Community Leader, and announcement viewing for the Community Viewer.
      Overall, the combination of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, and MySQL provides a stable, cost-effective, and scalable solution for developing the proposed web-based system. This technology stack supports efficient system performance, secure data handling, and a user- friendly interface, ensuring that the platform effectively meets the needs of all user roles during the Ramadan season.

Tasks are moved to 'In Progress' once work begins and transferred to 'Completed' upon completion, allowing the team to effectively track workflow and project progress. Regular reviews of the Kanban board were conducted to identify issues, adjust priorities, and improve productivity.
This approach ensures that all required tasks are properly organized and completed without omission during the development process. The proponents also assigned specific roles for documentation and system development to improve coordination, accountability, and efficiency within the team. In the initial phase, project objectives were established, and system diagrams such as Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs), Context Flow Diagrams (CFDs), and Use Case Diagrams were developed to guide the system's structure and functionality.
